About CodeMaker Buddy


Are you a kid who is interested in getting started with coding, or in making cool stuff using code and electronic hardware, but don't know where to get started?

Welcome to the CodeMaker Buddy website. It is a place where kids aged 6-14 can get started learning to code. You can then use your coding skills to control cool hardware projects. You can even share ideas, code and projects with other kids, and see what they are working on. This website is meant to get kids started with code, and show them what is possible. It is not an in-depth learning website for teaching kids coding! There are already many great websites for doing that, like Khan Academy or Code Academy. There are links to these and other websites in the Learning Resources pages.

Have a look around this website. There are simple coding projects to get you started with, or more advanced projects which use coding and hardware.

Do you have any questions or comments? Then simply register with this website and you can post messages and comments, share code with other kids, and hopefully get an answer to your questions.

Very Important: If you are aged 12 or under you must use your parents of guardians email when registering.

The coding section has fun example projects creted by me in Scratch, App Inventor, Processing and Python. All example projects have the source code The maker section has projects with Arduino, and BBC Microbit. There is a forum where kids can blog, share ideas, discuss projects and learn together. The Buddy section allows kids to post messages and comments. In order to post kids must register with their parent's/guardian's permission if they are under 13 years of age. The Share section has a code snippet sharing facility, where kids can add snippets of code, which other kids may find useful. There are many video tutorials some of which created by me to help kids get started. There are other sections on Famous Coders and Famous Makers to help inspire kids. There are useful links to top learning resources Reviews of useful technology books and some cool STEM toys and kits which I have used/built. Irish coder and maker events and competitions like Dublin Maker, and Coolest Projects and Eir Junior Spiders.

Hi Kids, I'm Benjamin, creator of the CodeMaker Buddy website. I am 10 years old and live in Dublin, Ireland, and I love to code.

Ever since I was a little kid, I was always trying to figure out how things worked. I started off on Lego® and Meccano®, building lots of challenging models, before asking my parents for a Lego® Mindstorms Robot. Unfortunately, I didn't get the Mindstorms Robot, but I did get an Arduino and later a Raspberry Pi.

I began coding when I was 6 years old (a long time ago!), starting off with the Scratch 1.4 programming language on the Raspberry Pi. I was very pleased to win the Best Educational Content Award at the annual Irish National Scratch competition in early 2015, with my project Snapshot Of our World. You can see it here. I joined CoderDojo in Summer 2015 at the Science Gallery, before we moved to Coderdojo Dublin @ the Warehouse in Zalando across the Quay from Google Headquarters.

At CoderDojo, I learned how to code websites and mobile devices, before moving onto Raspberry Pi and Python. I attended Coolest Projects 2016. Some of the kids from my Dojo have made some fantastic inventions, and won awards. I was lucky enough to pick up a runner up award in the Web Design Category. After that I started working on this website, originally called The MAKER Kid, and now called CodeMaker Buddy.

I was thrilled to be invited by CoderDojo to showcase my Maker Kid project at the RDS BTYSTE exhibition. You can read my blog here My Day at BTYSTE here. Since then, a coding club has started at my school, and I am helping out with the mentoring my schoolmates. Besides coding, I enjoy swimming, tennis, chess and of course Minecraft.

As you can see from one of the above tabs, I am a big Minecraft fan.
