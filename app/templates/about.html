About The MAKER Kid


Are you a kid who is interested in learning to code, or in making cool stuff using electronics, but don't know where to get started?

Welcome to The MAKER Kid website, a place where kids aged 6-14 can get together and learn about making stuff. You can even share ideas, code and projects with other kids.

Have a look around this website. There are simple coding projects to get started with, or more advanced projects which use coding and electronics. Interested in getting involved, or don't really understand how some of the projects work?

Then simply register with this website and you can post messages and comments, share code with other kids, and hopefully get an answer to your questions.

Hi Kids, I'm Benjamin, creator of The MAKER Kid website. I am 10 years old and live in Dublin, Ireland. I AM NOT THE MAKER KID. The MAKER Kid is any kid who is interested in learning code and using electronics and code to make stuff.

Ever since I was a little kid, I was always trying to figure out how things work. I started off on Lego® and Meccano®, building lots of challenging models, before asking my parents for a Lego® Mindstorms Robot.

Unfortunately, I didn't get the Mindstorms Robot, but I did get an Arduino and later a Raspberry Pi. As you can see from the picture on the left I am a big Minecraft fan. I come from a family of makers. My Mum loves making costumes, and my Dad loves tinkering with Electronics.

I began coding when I was 6 years old (a long time ago!), starting off with the Scratch programming language on the Raspberry Pi. I won the Best Educational Content Award at the annual Irish National Scratch competition in 2015, with my project Snapshot Of our World. I joined Coderdojo in Summer 2015 originally at the Science Gallery, before we moved to Coderdojo Dublin @ the Warehouse in Zalando across the Quay from Google Headquarters.

At Coderdojo, I learned how to code websites. and mobile devices, before moving onto Raspberry Pi and Python. I attended Coolest Projects 2016 Coderdojo Coolest Projects 2016. After that I started working on this website --- The MAKER Kid.

I was invited by Coderdojo to showcase my Maker Kid project at the RDS BTYSTE exhibition. You can read my blog here My Day at BTYSTE here. A coding club has started at my school, and I am helping out with the mentoring.

Besides Coding, I enjoy Swimming, Tennis, Chess and of course Minecraft.

I wanted to show other kids that coding and making stuff is fun. If kids try some of the projects they will soon realise that making stuff with code is great fun and that there are great technology resources in our own country.

I wanted to use all the stuff that I learned in both CoderDojo and at home, under one big project.

I wanted to establish links with other kids who are interested in making stuff, and provide a forum where we could communicate and learn from each other.

I wanted to learn other skills like making videos and editing images.

My website is built by a kid, for Irish kids and kids all around the world, with relevant help and guidelines on how to get started and have fun with all areas of making stuff with code.


This website was hand coded from scratch using the all the skills I learned at CoderDojo @ Warehouse, and on various websites.

I built this website using CSS/Bootstrap, HTML, JavaScript/JQuery, Python, and Flask which a micro web framework written in Python. There is a mySQL database wich stores the Posts, comments and code snippet.



If you are wondering what these languages are, then check out some of the links on the the Coding section of the Website. Also you can register and add a post with any questions.
