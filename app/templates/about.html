About The MAKER Kid


Are you a kid who is interested in making cool stuff like robots and other projects, but don't know where to get started?

The MAKER Kid is a learning website where kids aged 6-14 can get together and learn about making stuff. You can even share ideas and projects with other kids.

Have a look around this website. There are simple projects to get started with, or more advanced projects which use coding and electronics.

Interested in getting involved, or don't really understand how some of the projects work? Then simply register with this website and you can post messages and comments, share code with other kids, and hopefully get an answer to your questions.

Hi Kids, I'm Benjamin, creator of The MAKER Kid website. I am 10 years old and live in Dublin, Ireland. I AM NOT THE MAKER KID. The MAKER Kid is any kid that is interested in making stuff, and visits this Website... like what you are doing right now!!

Since I was a little kid, I was always trying to figure out how things work. I started off on Lego® and Meccano®, building lots of challenging models, before asking my parents for a Lego® Mindstorms Robot. Unfortunately, I didn't get the Mindstorms Robot, but I did get an Arduino and later a Raspberry Pi. As you can see from the picture from the previous tab, I am a big Minecraft fan. I come from a family of makers. My Mum loves making costumes, and my Dad loves tinkering with Electronics.

I began coding when I was 6 years old (a long time ago!), starting off with the Scratch programming language on the Raspberry Pi. I won the Best Educational Content Award at the annual Scratch competition in 2015, with my project Snapshot Of our World. I joined Coderdojo in Summer 2015 originally at the Science Gallery, before we moved to Coderdojo Dublin @ the Warehouse in Zalando beside Google Headquarters.

At Coderdojo, I learned how to code websites. and mobile devices, before moving onto Raspberry Pi and Python.

I was invited by Coderdojo to showcase my Maker Kid project at the RDS BTYSTE exhibition. You can read my blog My Day at BTYSTE here.

Besides Coding, I enjoy Swimming, Tennis, Golf, Chess and of course Minecraft.

I love making stuff and wanted to show other kids that making stuff is fun. If kids try some of my exercises they will soon realise that making stuff is great fun and there are great resources in our own country.

I wanted to use all the stuff that I learned in both CoderDojo and individually, under one big project.

I wanted to establish links with other kids who are interested in making stuff, and provide a forum where they could communicate and learn from each other.

My Mum wanted me to design a website for our Local Residents' Assocation but I decided that I should start with one for kids like me. I wanted to learn other skills like making videos and editing images.

My website is built by a kid, for Irish kids and kids all around the world, with relevant hints, ideas and guidelines on how to get started and have fun with all areas of making stuff.


This website was hand coded from scratch using the skills I learned at CoderDojo @ Warehouse, and on various websites.

I built this website using CSS/Bootstrap, HTML, JavaScript/JQuery, Python and SQLite (change to new sql database later). The Server is based on the Flask Framework which is a lightweight Python Micro framework.



If you are wondering what these languages are, then check out some of the links on the the Coding section of the Website.
